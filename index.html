Games for the Overlooked Middle

Luovanori makes games for the people nobody makes games for. We design tabletop and journaling experiences that name what modern life actually feels like — the pressure, the guilt, the exhaustion of being the person everyone depends on.

Our debut title, Unfolding Quest: Reclaim the Margin, is a solo journaling RPG for the sandwich generation: adults who care for their children and their aging parents simultaneously, while trying to hold themselves together. It is a protocol for survival disguised as a game.

Design Pillars

1

Radical Honesty

The game does not soften what burnout feels like. Every mechanic — Energy depletion, permanent Burdens, the Cold Boot — mirrors the real cost of being a caretaker. Naming the problem is the first step toward addressing it.

2

Refusal as Recovery

Mainstream wellness culture asks you to add more. We ask you to subtract. The core mechanic of Reclaim the Margin is the Redaction — the physical act of crossing out an obligation and reclaiming that space for yourself.

3

Play as Mirror

The dice rolls, the Shadow encounters, the weekly grid — they are all prompts for excavation. This is not fiction. The game asks you to write one real sentence about what drained you today. That sentence is the healing.

Unfolding Quest: Reclaim the Margin

PROPERTY OF LUOVANORI, INC. // COGNITIVE SOLUTIONS DIVISION // CLASSIFIED: LEVEL 4

You are the Anchor — the structural pivot of your family. Children depend on you above. Parents depend on you below. This is the Sandwich Biome, and you are caught in the middle.

Reclaim the Margin is a week-long solo journaling protocol. The goal is not to eliminate the pressure. The goal is to protect the small spaces — the Margins — where you still exist as yourself.

The 21-Slot Grid

Your week is divided into 21 slots. Sixteen are Siege — the obligations that press in from every direction. Five are yours. The protocol is built around protecting them.
